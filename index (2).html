Why this matters

The PI-RADS problem
no one talks about.

Studies show significant variability in PI-RADS classification between radiologists — especially at categories 3 and 4. The wrong reading can mean an unnecessary biopsy, or a missed cancer.

PI-RADS 3 vs 4 — High Stakes

The boundary between active surveillance and biopsy. An expert with 15,000+ cases reads this differently than a generalist.

No Subspecialist Access

Most hospitals have no radiologist specialised in Prostate MRI with high case volume. General readings miss the nuance.

Patient Uncertainty

An unclear PI-RADS score creates anxiety and delays treatment. A definitive expert second opinion changes everything.
